Habitable Exoplanets

About

Did you all ever wonder about “What is beyond the stars?”. Well, we’ve decided that, far beyond the stars, at approximately 4 lightyears from our home on planet Earth, there is an exoplanet called…. In this exoplanet, life conditions are harsh and a bit unknown, but not mismatched with the conditions here on Earth. That’s why human mankind has decided to take a step on far space exploration and send on a mission to a number of people willing to know if there would be a chance to survive on this exoplanet.

Prospera Sustentia

  • Planet Type: Super Earth
  • Discovery Date: 2016
  • Mass: 1.07 Earth
  • Planet Radius: 1.03 x Earth (estimate)
  • Orbital Radius: 0.04856 AU
  • Orbital Period: 11.2 days
  • Eccentricity: 0.02
  • Detection Method: Radial Velocity

Facts

Fortunately, the concept of open science spread around our home planet and scientists all over the world investigated sharing their findings. Thanks to that collaboration, they discovered Prospera Sustentia, a Super Earth type planet with a synchronous orbit around a yellow dwarf a little younger than the Sun, approximately 4 lightyears away from Earth. The fact that the conditions here were so like Earth's made it pretty easy to be honest. There’s plenty of water, oxygen is present in a 17% in the air and most of the rest is Nitrogen also. Besides, we can count on solar energy as well as wind power. We are still considering our options around geothermal and hydro power sources.
